The Story of Aarohan

It Started with One Child

In 2004, our founder Ms. Rani Patel encountered a 7-year-old rag picker named Rohan on the streets of Delhi. Learning about street children's struggles with substance abuse and exploitation, she and colleague Anjana Tata decided to bring these children into the educational network — and Aarohan was born.

Our Journey

From that single act of compassion, Aarohan has grown into a movement touching thousands of lives. Today, we work across Delhi NCR, Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, and Jharkhand, serving children, women, and marginalized communities through education, healthcare, skill training, and community development.

Our Vision

For every child to have the opportunity to exercise her/his right to education, health, and nutrition — and for every adult to access a livelihood with dignity.

Education for All Health & Nutrition Livelihood with Dignity

Our Mission

To provide comprehensive and holistic support through education, health care, skill training, and other programs for children, women, transgender, and community members — aligning with Sustainable Development Goals.

Holistic Support SDG Aligned Community Focus

Our Values

Regardless of caste, creed, religion, or gender — we believe every individual deserves an equal opportunity to thrive and reach their full potential.

What We Do

Our Programs & Projects

Comprehensive programs tailored to community needs

Education

Early Childhood Care & Development

Since 2006, our ECCE centers provide holistic development for children ages 2-5 from underprivileged backgrounds. Our first center in Jagdamba Camp, South Delhi became a model replicated by Save the Children-Nepal, IGNOU, Manipal University, and University of Alabama.

  • Play-way teaching methods
  • Parent & caregiver training
  • Child protection awareness
Education

After School Hours

Academic support for school-going children through four specialized projects:

  • PROJECT PASS — Primary level support with mid-day meals
  • PROJECT PATHWAYS — Classes VI-VIII with Science & Math focus
  • PROJECT HOPE — Classes IX-X with Board exam preparation
  • PROJECT SHIKHAR — Classes XI-XII with career counseling
Empowerment

Women Empowerment — "Teach the Mother"

Launched in 2012, recognizing that a child's growth is proportionate to the strength of the mother. Partners with USHA Sili School for certification.

  • Fashion design & garment making
  • Digital & financial literacy
  • Entrepreneur skills training
  • Personal grooming workshops
Community

Tribal Community Development

Working with tribal communities in East Coast Belt — Odisha (Mayurbhanj), Andhra Pradesh (Vishakhapatnam, Srikakulam, Vizayanagram), and Jharkhand (Ranchi, Gamharia).

  • Education for tribal children
  • Health & hygiene programs
  • Livelihood skilling
  • Cultural preservation
Advocacy

Aarohan With 3rd Gender

Working for transgender rights through collaborations with individuals, organizations, and government bodies including the National Human Rights Commission.

  • Financial literacy workshops
  • Skill training in beauty culture
  • Personality development
  • Rights advocacy
Community

Community Library

Interactive libraries encouraging reading habits and literacy. Locations include Jammun Wala Park, Jagdamba Camp, government schools in Mangolpuri-Balbir Nagar, and Sakipur Village.

  • Storytelling sessions
  • Art, craft & music activities
  • Film screenings & sports
  • Safe learning spaces

Our Unique Methodology

All programs are developed in response to children's needs. The healthy growth of a child is possible with a conducive environment encompassing family, community, education, income, and dignity.

01

Play-Way Teaching

Audio-visual materials, role-playing, games, clay modeling, colors, and drawing enhance emotional and creative expression.

02

Leadership Building

Every child is given responsibilities — class monitor, food monitor — to build leadership qualities from an early age.

03

24×7 Parent Engagement

Teachers available via WhatsApp groups round the clock. Regular counseling sessions and monthly meetings with school teachers.

04

Trauma-Informed Care

Teachers trained to handle traumatized children. Special counseling support and POSCO E-Box awareness.

05

Bridge Education

Non-formal education and bridge classes for out-of-school children. Curriculum aligned to State/Central Board standards.

06

Nutrition & Health

Mid-day meals provided for proper nutrition. Regular health check-ups and hygiene education.
